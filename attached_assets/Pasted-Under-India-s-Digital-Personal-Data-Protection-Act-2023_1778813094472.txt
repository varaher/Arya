Under India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023, users have the legal right to:
Know what data is stored about them
Correct inaccurate data
Erase their data completely
Withdraw consent at any time
This isn't optional. It's the law.

That last one is the hardest. Derived insights built from deleted data — do they also get deleted? Legally and ethically, yes.
How to Build It Right
Three levels of forgetting:
1. Selective Forget
User says: "Forget everything I told you about my job"
ARYA removes only that context thread.
Useful for sensitive topics — relationships, health scares, financial stress.
2. Period Wipe
"Forget the last 3 months"
Useful if someone went through a difficult phase they don't want carried forward.
3. Full Reset
Complete deletion. Account, history, insights, everything.
Must be irreversible and confirmed clearly.
The UX Opportunity Hidden Here
Most apps treat data deletion as a painful exit flow buried in settings.
ARYA can make it a feature.
Imagine ARYA saying:
"It's been 6 months. Some of what I know about you is from a harder chapter. Want to let go of any of it?"
This reframes deletion as intentional growth — shedding an old version of yourself — which fits perfectly with ARYA's identity as a growth companion.
You could even call it something other than "delete."
"Let Go" — for selective memory removal
"New Chapter" — for a period wipe
"Fresh Start" — for full reset
Technical Reality
You need to architect this from Day 1 — not retrofit it later.
Key requirements:
Every piece of data tagged with user ID + timestamp + category
Deletion must cascade — primary data, backups, derived insights, logs
Confirmation within 72 hours of request — DPDP requirement
Audit trail that deletion happened — without storing the deleted content
The Trust Dividend
Here's the counterintuitive truth —
The easier you make it to leave, the more people trust you enough to stay.
If users know they can wipe everything instantly, they share more openly. And sharing more openly makes ARYA smarter. Which makes them less likely to actually leave.
Right to Forget isn't a compliance checkbox. It's a trust-building product feature.
Make it visible, make it easy, and talk about it in your onboarding.
